What is meant by Arthritis? What are it's causes?

  • Osteoarthritis is a degenerative joint disease that occurs when the protective cartilage that cushions the ends of bones within a joint gradually wears down over time.
  • As the cartilage breaks down, bones within the joint may begin to rub against each other, causing pain, stiffness, and swelling.
  • The condition is most prevalent in older adults, but it can also affect younger people who have had previous joint injuries or who engage in repetitive joint movements.
  • Risk factors for developing osteoarthritis include age, genetics, obesity, joint injuries, and certain medical conditions such as diabetes and gout.
  • While there is no cure for osteoarthritis, treatment options can help to manage pain and improve joint function, which may include physical therapy, medication, weight loss, and in some cases, surgery.
  • Fact: Osteoarthritis is the second most common rheumatologic problem and it is the most frequent joint disease with a prevalence of 22% to 39% in India. OA is more common in women than men.
